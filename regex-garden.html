Syntax. Atoms are single letters or digits, and the symbol ε for the empty string. Operators, in order of binding strength, are *, +, ? (postfix), implicit concatenation, and | (union). Parentheses group as usual. So (a|b)*abb is read as “any number of a’s and b’s, followed by the literal abb”.

How generation works. The expression is parsed into a syntax tree and then converted to a Thompson NFA with ε‑transitions. We enumerate every string over the regex’s alphabet up to the chosen maximum length and keep the ones the NFA accepts.

How equivalence works. Two languages are equal iff they agree on every string. We can’t test infinitely many strings, but for a fixed depth k, if R₁ and R₂ disagree on some string of length ≤ k we display it as a witness; if they agree on all of them, they are equivalent up to length k. In practice, depth 6–8 is usually convincing for simple patterns!
